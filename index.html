Travel Adventures

The purpose of this project exam was to apply the skills acquired during the first year of our studies in front-end development. The exam aimed to assess our ability to interpret a project brief and develop a website that delivers value to stakeholders. Our task was to utilize our knowledge of HTML, JavaScript, and CSS to build a static HTML website. The website needed to be responsive, accessible, and tailored to meet the specific requirements of our stakeholders.

Science Museum

This project showcases my creative and technical skills acquired during the first semester of my studies at Noroff. The task was to design and develop a website for the Community Science Museum, an interactive museum aimed at primary and middle school children (7-15) and families with young children. The challenge was to create an informative and engaging website that appeals to youngsters while assuming their inquisitive and intelligent nature. The website needed to encourage visitors to explore the museum in person and be easily accessible on various devices.

Using HTML and CSS, I crafted a responsive website that utilizes media assets, including text and images, to present information in an organized and visually appealing manner. I also incorporated CSS animations to enhance the user experience.

Rainy Days

Rainydays is a fictional e-commerce website that I developed as a project at Noroff. The objective was to create an engaging online platform aimed at selling raincoats to individuals between the ages of 30 and 60, with a mid-range salary level. The website was designed to showcase a variety of raincoat options and provide a seamless shopping experience for the target audience. The project incorporated HTML and CSS to ensure a visually appealing and functional website, offering a range of raincoat styles to suit different preferences and needs.
